### Human Verification Required

**1. NLP Extraction Accuracy on Real GDELT Headlines**

**Test:** Monitor live `/api/news` responses and inspect `actor`/`action`/`target` fields on returned articles. Look for headlines where extraction is nonsensical or misleading.

**Expected:** Conflict headlines like "Israel strikes Hezbollah positions in Lebanon" should extract actor=Israel, action=strikes, target=Lebanon/Hezbollah. Diplomatic headlines like "Tehran condemns US sanctions" should extract condemns (not a conflict verb) and thus score below 0.7.

**Why human:** NLP accuracy on real-world headlines requires manual spot-checking against live GDELT data. Automated tests cover synthetic cases; real headlines have diverse syntax patterns that may confuse the POS tagger.

**2. False Positive Rate Improvement**

**Test:** Compare news feed article quality before and after deployment. Check whether ambiguous articles (sports, entertainment, historical) still appear in the `/api/news` response.

**Expected:** Significant reduction in non-conflict articles; conflict-relevant articles should dominate the feed.

**Why human:** Qualitative judgment of "relevant vs. irrelevant" at scale requires reading actual returned articles over time.

**3. Threshold Calibration (0.7 default)**

**Test:** Monitor whether the 0.7 threshold is too aggressive (filters out legitimate conflict news) or too permissive (still passes irrelevant articles) over 24-48 hours of live data.

**Expected:** Threshold should allow direct conflict reporting through while blocking diplomatic/context-only articles. If too many legitimate articles are filtered, lower via `NEWS_RELEVANCE_THRESHOLD` env var.

**Why human:** The correct threshold depends on real GDELT data distribution which can only be assessed operationally.
