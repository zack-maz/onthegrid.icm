## Human Verification Required

### 1. Geographic layer elevation tinting

**Test:** Open the app, toggle the Geographic layer on in the layer panel
**Expected:** Monochrome elevation tinting appears over the map — dark blue-grey at sea level, slate at 1500m, lighter at 4000m+ — at 25% opacity over terrain
**Why human:** The `color-relief` layer type is not in react-maplibre 8.1.0 type definitions and uses `type as any`. MapLibre runtime support for `color-relief` must be visually confirmed

### 2. Contour line zoom behavior

**Test:** With Geographic layer active, zoom the map to level 7, then 9+
**Expected:** At zoom 7 faint contour lines appear; at zoom 9+ they become clearly visible showing terrain contours across mountain ranges
**Why human:** Zoom-gated tile rendering requires a live MapLibre context

### 3. Geographic feature labels at zoom tiers

**Test:** With Geographic layer active, pan/zoom from z4 to z8+
**Expected:** At z4: Zagros Mountains, Persian Gulf, Caspian Sea labels appear. At z6: Dasht-e Kavir, Elburz Mountains, Red Sea etc. added. At z8: Tigris River, Strait of Hormuz, etc. added
**Why human:** MapLibre filter expression `['<=', ['get', 'minzoom'], ['zoom']]` behavior requires live render to confirm

### 4. Elevation legend display

**Test:** Toggle Geographic layer on, observe bottom-left map corner
**Expected:** Elevation legend card appears showing 3 color stops: dark (#1a1a2e) = 0m, slate (#334155) = 1500m, light grey (#94a3b8) = 4000m
**Why human:** MapLegend renders conditionally based on runtime active layer state

### 5. Terrain exaggeration unchanged

**Test:** With terrain visible (any zoom), toggle Geographic layer on and off
**Expected:** Mountain relief appears equally steep in both states — 3.0x exaggeration is not altered by the geographic layer toggle
**Why human:** Visual quality check for terrain depth perception

### 6. Weather heatmap rendering

**Test:** Toggle the Weather layer on
**Expected:** Smooth temperature gradient heatmap covers the Middle East region (15-42N / 30-70E) — blue over cold mountainous areas, green/yellow over moderate zones, red over hot desert regions
**Why human:** HeatmapLayer uses WebGL shader-based rendering not testable in jsdom

### 7. Wind barbs visual density and direction

**Test:** With Weather layer active, pan across the Middle East at a comfortable zoom level
**Expected:** Wind barbs appear at approximately 3-degree spacing (roughly every few hundred km), oriented to show wind direction with classic meteorological barb notation
**Why human:** IconLayer with `getAngle` rotation requires live WebGL rendering

### 8. Weather tooltip on hover

**Test:** With Weather layer active, move cursor slowly over the heatmap area
**Expected:** Tooltip appears near cursor showing: temperature in C and F (e.g., "28.3C / 82.9F"), wind as compass direction + speed (e.g., "Wind: NW 12 kn")
**Why human:** Requires deck.gl picking pipeline with live WebGL context

### 9. Temperature legend display

**Test:** Toggle Weather layer on, observe bottom-left map corner
**Expected:** Temperature legend card shows 4-stop gradient: blue (-5C/23F) through green, yellow, to red (45C/113F)
**Why human:** Same as elevation legend — runtime active layer check in MapLegend
